Family 04 · Gear Bite
The wedge that implies motion.
A full gear with a triangular slice removed. The bite creates directionality without adding an explicit arrow — the eye fills in the motion. Below: symmetric, amber-arrow, and chevron treatments of the same cut.
Parent · 04
04A
Symmetric Bite

Clean pie-wedge cut from 12 o'clock to 3 o'clock. The cut goes all the way to the hub so the hole is part of the outline, not a separate element. Most legible at small sizes.

Fixars
04B
Arrow Bite

The wedge is filled with amber instead of cut — reads as a sharp arrow rising out of the gear. Pops at every size, but the amber becomes load-bearing.

Fixars
04C
Chevron Bite

Two thin slashes cut through the gear body like a fast-forward symbol. Most editorial and contemporary of the three — sacrifices some legibility for personality.

Fixars
Family 05 · Fixed ✓
The most literal mark — "fix" made visual.
A gear holding a checkmark says exactly what Fixars does. Three treatments: inverted solid, slim editorial, and a dual-ring badge that reads like a trust seal.
Parent · 05
05A
Inverted Solid

The gear is solid and the checkmark is the negative space. Most emphatic at small sizes — the check reads even at 20px. A single-color mark, no amber required.

Fixars
05B
Slim Editorial

Hairline gear ring, check drawn in the same weight, with an amber dot marking the finish point. Most refined and contemporary — pairs beautifully with Space Grotesk at any scale ≥ 32px.

Fixars
05C
Trust-Seal Badge

Gear wrapped in a quiet outer ring — reads as a certification / verified-by seal. Powerful for trust-heavy surfaces (KYC, escrow, stake-verified badges) but busier at small sizes.

Fixars
Family 06 · Four-Tooth
Four teeth = four sub-apps.
The gear becomes a diagram of Fixars' architecture — four cardinal teeth for the four products (Social, Business, Learning, Payments), F in the hub. Three treatments: square teeth, rotated diamond, and network nodes.
Parent · 06
06A
Square Teeth

Tightened proportions of the parent — slightly smaller teeth, cleaner F inside the disc. Looks like a compass rose for the ecosystem. Most stable and institutional of the three.

Fixars
06B
Diamond Teeth

Teeth rotated 45° into diamonds, with one amber to mark a "current" app. More dynamic, feels like an interface element — works well as a live status indicator in-product.

Fixars
06C
Network Nodes

Teeth become circular nodes connected to the central disc. Reads as an ecosystem / network diagram rather than mechanical — softer, more platform-feeling, less engineering-coded.

Fixars
If Fixars is an infrastructure play
Pick from Family 04 (Gear Bite). The solid-gear-with-a-wedge reads as "we build the machinery that moves money forward." Sharp, confident, engineering-first.
If Fixars is a trust / verification play
Pick from Family 05 (Fixed ✓). Especially 05B (slim) for the public brand or 05C (seal) for compliance-heavy surfaces. The check does a lot of semantic work for you.
If Fixars is an ecosystem / super-app play
Pick from Family 06 (Four-Tooth). The four teeth literalize the four sub-apps in the Blueprint — every time someone sees the mark they see the architecture.
