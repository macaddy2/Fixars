01 · Three treatments

One mark, three contexts.

The gradient carries hero and marketing surfaces; the solid is the workhorse for product UI, docs and co-marks; the monochrome outline handles print, watermarks, embossing and any low-fidelity context.

Treatment · 01
Gradient — hero
Cyan → blue → deep blue diagonal gradient. Use on white/paper marketing surfaces, hero banners, pitch decks, large product moments. Never below 64px.
Treatment · 02
Solid — workhorse
Flat Fixars blue with white F cutout. The default in product UI, docs, partner co-marks, and anywhere the gradient would be noisy. Works from 16px up.
Treatment · 03
Monochrome — stamp
Outlined 1-color version for print, watermarks, regulatory filings, and embossed/foil applications. Never tint — full ink or full white only.

Why this mark, for Fixars.

Across five original sketches, one motif recurred: a gear with something inside it. That intuition was right. A gear says "fix" more immediately than a shield (defense), a block (modularity), or an arrow (growth).

The F lives inside the gear because Fixars is the letter inside the machine — the operating system of African commerce, not a product sitting next to it. Gear + F is a brand thesis, not a decoration.

The gradient (cyan → blue → deep blue) is a nod to motion and depth without resorting to motion-blur clichés. The solid variant keeps it legal-safe, print-safe, and small-size-safe.
